Player-feedback / ability / UI samples (sfx_fb_<event>.wav, sfx_fb_<event>_1.wav, ...)
  Files:   sfx_fb_hitmarker*, sfx_fb_hit_crit*, sfx_fb_kill*, sfx_fb_kill_elite*,
           sfx_fb_dash*, sfx_fb_jump*, sfx_fb_ability_tactical*, sfx_fb_ability_ultimate*,
           sfx_fb_charge_ready*, sfx_fb_explosion*, sfx_fb_shield_absorb*,
           sfx_fb_shield_break*, sfx_fb_low_health*, sfx_fb_pickup_health*,
           sfx_fb_pickup_shield*, sfx_fb_pickup_ammo*, sfx_fb_pickup_scrap*,
           sfx_fb_pickup_powerup*, sfx_fb_ui_move*, sfx_fb_ui_confirm*, sfx_fb_ui_cancel*,
           sfx_fb_ui_reward*, sfx_fb_run_win*, sfx_fb_run_lose*
  Source:  Original procedural synthesis (no external recordings) by
           tools/audio/pulse_player_sfx_producer.py.
  License: Original work, no third-party rights.
  Notes:   These are the PLAYER bus: short tactile cues the player reads to confirm
           their own actions and state (hits, crits, kills, dash/jump, tactical/ultimate
           abilities, charge-ready, explosions, shield absorb/break, low-health warning,
           per-kind pickups, menu/reward navigation, run win/lose). The current pass
           avoids arcade coin/chime/fanfare language and favors filtered transients,
           body, air, servo, metal, and shield-fracture layers. Authored as deterministic
           round-robin banks and kept distinct from the weapon/enemy/world banks so a
           confirm never reads as a threat. The legacy engine one-shots
           (src/Engine/Audio.cpp) remain as the never-silent fallback when a feedback
           bank is absent. See docs/PULSE_PLAYER_AUDIO_SPEC.md.

All other utility SFX (hit, kill, dash, dryfire, pickup, hurt) are procedurally
synthesized by the engine (src/Engine/Audio.cpp) as the fallback layer under the
authored banks above.

Audio QA / validation
  tools/audio/pulse_audio_validate.py measures peak / RMS / true-peak / clipping for
  every bank and full-mix capture and FAILS on any digital clipping; it also emits PNG
  spectrograms. Build.bat runs it as a zero-clip mix gate on every build.
